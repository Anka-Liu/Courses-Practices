Suppose random variables X1, . . . , Xn are independent and identicially distributed (i.i.d.) N(0, 1). What are the mean and variance of X¯ = (X1 + . . . Xn)/n? You need to show the steps to get the conclusion¶

$\begin{align} E(\overline{X})&=E(\frac{X_1+X_2+...+X_n}{n})\\ &=\frac{E(X_1+X_2+...+X_n)}{n}\\ &=\frac{E(X_1)+E(X_2)+...+E(X_n)}{n}\\ &=\frac{0+0+...+0}{n}\\ &=0 \end{align}$

$\begin{align} Var(\overline{X})&=Var(\frac{X_1+X_2+...+X_n}{n})\\ &=\frac{Var(X_1+X_2+...+X_n)}{n^2}\\ &=\frac{Var(X_1)+Var(X_2)+...+Var(X_n)}{n^2}\\ &=\frac{1+1+...+1}{n^2}\\ &=\frac{1}{n} \end{align}$
